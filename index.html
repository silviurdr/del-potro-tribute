Juan Martin Del Potro

Argentinian tennis player

Juan Martín del Potro Lucas (born 23 September 1988), nicknamed Delpo, is an Argentine professional tennis player. Del Potro is an offensive baseliner with a very powerful serve and deep, flat, topspin groundstroke, where he pushes his opponents deeper with his heavy groundstroke and trying to blast past them with a winner. His forehand is one of his main strengths and possibly the most powerful in the game, capable of frequently generating speeds more than 160 km/h.

Career Highlights - Year By Year

  • 2019 -- Played only 5 events due to right knee injury, which required surgery in Barcelona on 22 June.
  • 2018 -- Achieved career-high No. 3 in ATP Rankings on 13 August - 4 wrist surgeries and 8 years after becoming No. 4. Upset No. 1 Nadal in US Open SF to reach 2nd Grand Slam final 9 years after 1st (lost to Djokovic). Set all-time record with 21 Grand Slam appearances between finals. Saved 3 championship points to defeat No. 1 Federer for 1st ATP Masters 1000 title at Indian Wells. Now owns 10 wins over World No. 1 - most among all players who’ve never been ranked No. 1 themselves.
  • 2017 -- Successfully defended Stockholm title to win 20th ATP World Tour championship of career (defeated Dimitrov). Fell one set shy of qualifying for Nitto ATP Finals, losing 3-setter to Isner in ATP Masters 1000 Paris QF.
  • 2016 -- Del Potro did not drop a set en route to Stockholm title as WC (defeated Sock) - 1st championship in 33 months (2014 Sydney). As World No. 141, upset No. 1 Djokovic and No. 5 Nadal en route to Rio Olympics final (lost to No. 2 Murray in 4h2m). Defeated Karlovic, then rallied from 2 sets down for 1st time to beat No. 6 Cilic in Davis Cup final (ARG defeated CRO 3-2).
  • 2015 -- Only played 4 matches all season. Underwent left wrist surgeries on January 20 and June 18 at Mayo Clinic in Rochester, MN, USA. Announced split with longtime coach Franco Davin on July 24
  • 2014 -- The Argentine finished outside Top 100 for 1st time since 2005 after contesting just 4 tournaments during year.
  • 2013-- Finished No. 5 for 1st time since ‘09 and in Top 10 for 4th time in 6 years, winning 4 titles at 500 level – Rotterdam, Washington, Tokyo and Basel. Won 50-more matches for 3rd time in his career and finished season strong with 16-4 record since 2R at US Open (lost to Hewitt).
  • 2012 -- Had 1st Top 10 finish since ’09 and won career-high 65 matches, most for an Argentine since Coria won 60 in ‘03. Won 4 titles in 5 finals.
  • 2011 -- Del Potro returned from a right wrist injury to earn ATP Comeback Player of Year as he captured 2 titles, reached another final and 4 SF en route to jump of 246 spots, biggest in Top 20. Led his country to Davis Cup final with 2 wins in SF tie in Serbia, defeating Tipsarevic and No. 1 Djokovic, who retired with a back injury…In final vs. Spain, lost in 5 sets to Ferrer and 4 sets to Nadal.
  • 2010 -- The Tandil native only played three tournaments due to right wrist injury.
  • 2009 -- The top South American on ATP World Tour captured three titles, including his first Grand Slam crown, and reached his first ATP World Tour Masters 1000 final…Closed season by reaching final at Barclays ATP World Tour Finals in London (lost to Davydenko). At US Open he captured title with back-to-back wins over No. 3 Nadal in SF and No. 1 Federer in final in five sets (rallying from 1-2 set deficit)…Became first player to beat Nadal and Federer in a Grand Slam tournament and first Argentine to capture US Open since Vilas in 1977 Went 11-9 versus Top 10 opponents and earned a career-high $4,753,087.
  • 2008 -- The youngest player in the year-end Top 10, won four titles in five finals. Finished as top Argentine and South American for first time and also helped his country to Davis Cup final (lost to Spain 3-1). Won first ATP title in Stuttgart (defeating Gasquet) and following week won in Kitzbnhel (defeating Melzer), both on clay. Moved to hard courts and won back-to-back titles in Los Angeles (defeated Roddick) and Washington (defeated Troicki) before streak ended with QF loss at US Open (lost to Murray).
  • 2007 -- Finished as youngest player in Top 50 at 19 years, 2 months. In October at AMS Madrid, defeated No. 9 Robredo for first Top 10 win en route to 3rd RD (lost to eventual champ Nalbandian). Went 1-9 vs. Top 10 opponents and compiled marks of 21-19 on hard, 4-3 on grass and 1-2 on clay.
  • 2006 -- Youngest player to finish in Top 100 during year at 18 years, 2 months. Made his debut on ATP circuit and posted two ATP QF. Also won a pair of Challenger titles at Aguascalientes (d. Roitman)and Segovia (d. Verdasco in QF, Becker in F) to compile a 17-10 Challenger mark. Qualified five times into main draw of ATP level tournaments.

Best Moments

Juan Martin del Potro, a 20 year-old from Argentina, won his first grand slam title by coming back from two sets to one down for a five-set victory over Roger Federer in the US Open final. Del Potro's success in 2009 also ended Federer's five-year winning streak at the US Open.

Juan Martins' forehand earned him a nickname “Del Thortro” from Roger Federer, referring to the superhero Thor. “It was funny to watch. Every player was talking about my forehand and my new nickname on tour. Then I got a couple of hammers on the tour. I like this new nickname on me and still working hard on my forehands.”

Juan Martin del Potro was brought to tears after an incredible show of support from fans during his US Open quarter-final against Stan Wawrinka. As del Potro went out for the last game, the fans that had remained as the clock moved past 1:00am serenaded him and the big Argentine wiped away tears as "Ole, ole, ole, ole, Delpo, Delpo," rained down from the Arthur Ashe Stadium stands.
